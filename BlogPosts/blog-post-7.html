Week 7: UI and UX Analysis — Takealot.com vs Amazon.co.za

By Jordyn Van Aswegen

Posted : March 31, 2025

UI and UX Analysis — Takealot.com vs Amazon.co.za

This analysis explores the user interface (UI) and user experience (UX) of two major e-commerce platforms operating in South Africa: Takealot and Amazon. While both serve a similar purpose, their design approaches, functionality, and user interactions differ in notable ways.

Takealot.com: Established and Familiar

Takealot.com is one of South Africa’s most widely used online shopping platforms. It has developed a solid reputation for ease of use, efficient navigation, and generally intuitive design.

Colour Scheme and Visual Hierarchy

The website employs a clean white background with blue accents, contributing to a minimalistic and modern design. The colour contrast between interface elements, such as buttons and text, is effective in drawing attention to key actions. This enhances usability by reducing cognitive load and allowing users to locate elements with ease.

Search Functionality

Strengths: The search bar is prominently placed at the top of the homepage, facilitating immediate access for users. It offers intelligent suggestions based on past browsing history and common queries, which adds a sense of personalisation and streamlines the user’s shopping journey.

Areas for Improvement: Search results are not consistently accurate. The current algorithm occasionally yields unrelated products, which can disrupt the user experience and lead to frustration. Refining the algorithm to better understand keyword intent would improve efficiency.

Product Recommendations

Takealot successfully uses browsing history to present relevant product suggestions. Users are able to quickly return to previously viewed items, while related product suggestions help users discover complementary or alternative options. This feature enhances the platform’s user retention and product visibility.

Navigation and Layout

The site layout is intuitive and aligns with user expectations. Categories are well-organised, navigation is streamlined, and all key features are easy to locate without excessive searching. The footer is particularly well-structured, dividing information into logical sections such as "Shop Account", "Help", "Company", and "Policies", making customer support and legal resources easily accessible.

Accessibility

Takealot demonstrates a degree of accessibility through readable font sizes, generous spacing, and responsive design across multiple devices.

Opportunities for Improvement:

  • The inclusion of alternative (alt) text for product images would enhance compatibility with screen readers.
  • Adding keyboard navigation support would further accommodate users with limited motor function.

UX Highlights

  • Personalised product suggestions enhance engagement.
  • Smooth and familiar navigation supports a positive user journey.
  • The checkout process is efficient, which is critical for online retail success.

Overall Areas for Improvement

  • Improving the accuracy of the search algorithm.
  • Incorporating more inclusive design elements, such as alt text and keyboard navigation.

In summary, Takealot.com offers a reliable and user-friendly shopping experience. Its design is well-executed, although enhancements in search optimisation and accessibility would strengthen its overall performance.

Amazon.co.za: A New Entrant with Strong Foundations

Product Range and Brand Diversity

The platform offers a wide variety of products, including both international brands (such as Apple, HP, and Sony) and local South African brands (such as Amanda-Jayne, Masodi, and King Kong Leather). This blend appeals to a broad audience, catering to both global trends and local preferences.

Delivery and Logistics

The platform offers competitive delivery options, including same-day and next-day delivery for select items. In addition, customers benefit from free delivery on their first order and on subsequent orders exceeding R500. These features significantly enhance convenience and reflect Amazon’s logistical strength.

UI and UX Design

The interface closely resembles Amazon’s global design, with a strong focus on product recommendations and search functionality. While functional, the current layout can appear visually cluttered. This density of information may overwhelm new users or those less familiar with Amazon’s navigation patterns.

Accessibility and Support

Amazon.co.za provides a comprehensive range of customer support options, including phone support, email assistance, and live chat. The return policy is customer-centric, offering 30-day returns with either home pickup or self-drop-off options.

Impact on Local Business

Amazon’s global strategy of enabling third-party sellers is maintained on the South African site. With over 60% of Amazon’s international sales coming from independent sellers, this model has the potential to greatly benefit South African small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs), providing a scalable platform for growth and exposure.

UX Highlights

  • Efficient logistics enhance user convenience.
  • Product recommendations increase engagement and relevance.
  • Support for local businesses offers long-term economic value.

Areas for Improvement

  • The interface would benefit from a simplified layout to reduce visual clutter and improve navigation clarity.
  • Further refinement of the search functionality would improve the precision of product results and overall user satisfaction.

Conclusion

Both platforms offer strong e-commerce experiences tailored to the South African market. Takealot stands out for its intuitive navigation, clean interface, and local familiarity, though its search function and accessibility could be improved. Amazon, while newer, presents robust delivery options and supports local economic development through SME integration. However, it would benefit from a cleaner interface and refined search experience.

As the competition grows, each platform has the potential to improve its UX and UI offerings to better serve a diverse and evolving customer base.
