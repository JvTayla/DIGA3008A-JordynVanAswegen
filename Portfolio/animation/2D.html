2D Animations

Take Animation

For my final take animation, I wanted to push the boundaries and create something much more dynamic and impactful. This time, I focused on a transition from shocked to fear, and the result felt much more in line with the brief. The animation is filled with movement, not just in the facial expressions but in the body language, eye movement, and even subtle details like the tightening of muscles. The facial expressions shift smoothly, with shock quickly giving way to fear in a more dramatic and fluid way. This animation truly comes alive—there’s a sense of tension and urgency in every frame. The emotions feel raw, and the pacing reflects a more natural progression of fear. Overall, the animation is much more dynamic, showcasing how far I’ve come in capturing emotion with depth and fluidity.

Practice Take Animation

My animation journey began with a facial expression animation, where I was tasked with capturing the transition of emotions. I chose to animate the shift from anger to sadness, but the result didn’t quite meet the brief. The animation felt static, and although it conveyed the change in facial expression, it lacked the fluidity and dynamic energy I was aiming for. Despite this, I found joy in the subtler aspects of the animation—the tears, which added a layer of emotion, and the secondary movement of the hair and other elements. These movements gave the scene a touch of life that helped elevate the emotion in small but significant ways. While the animation wasn’t perfect, it was a great learning experience that pushed me to understand the importance of not only facial expression but the overall dynamics of the scene.
