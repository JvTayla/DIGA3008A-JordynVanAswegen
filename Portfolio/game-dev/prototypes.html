Cyber Nexus

Cyber Nexus is a character-swapping puzzle-environmental narrative game set in a high-tech research facility researching a revolutionary radiation cure. Players take control of Wisp and Aurora, two distinct characters, and must switch between them to overcome environmental challenges, solve intricate puzzles, and navigate the facility while finding out what happened to shut the facility down.

    • Divers Descent

    DiversDescent is a 2D platformer I created where I designed all the assets, from character animations to enemies, backgrounds, and power-ups. The game follows a marine biologist who stumbles upon a powerful energy source, which causes irregularities and hybrid mutations in the ocean. These mutations result in dangerous new creatures, including starfish-sea urchin, lionfish-pufferfish, and octopus-crab hybrids, that the diver must face as she embarks on her mission to retrieve the energy crystals and restore balance. As players navigate through underwater worlds, they encounter challenging platforming sequences, solve puzzles, and battle mutated sea creatures. With a strong focus on world-building and environmental storytelling, DiversDescent immerses players in a unique, hand-crafted oceanic world filled with danger and discovery.

  • Check it out here on Itch

    • Ratsky's Adventures - A working Title

    Ratsky Adventures (working title) is my latest project, a historical, environmental narrative stealth and heist game where players control Ratsky, a small but determined rat reclaiming stolen artifacts from the Brattish Museum and returning them to their rightful origins. The game blends stealth, parkour, and historical storytelling, offering an experience that challenges players to navigate complex spaces while uncovering hidden histories. This project is more than just a game—it's a way for me to refine my coding skills, experiment with immersive design, and push my understanding of game mechanics. As someone who loves environmental storytelling and games like Assassin’s Creed, I’m focusing on crafting a world that feels alive, rich with detail, and deeply engaging.

    Other Projects

    Inventory System

    One of my first coding projects was a fruit shop inventory simulation, where I built a basic system to manage inventory and funds using 2D Unity. This project helped me develop a foundational understanding of coding logic, resource management, and game systems. The simulation allowed players to track stock levels, sell items, and balance their funds, reinforcing key concepts like UI interactions, data management, and game economy mechanics. While simple, it was an important step in improving my coding skills and learning how to structure game systems effectively.

  • Check it out here on Itch

    • Space AirHockey

    Another one of my early coding projects was Space Air Hockey, a simple yet engaging 2D game built in Unity. This project focused on colliders, physics interactions, and fundamental coding mechanics, helping me improve my understanding of Unity’s physics system and how to create smooth-ish, responsive gameplay. By implementing collision detection, paddle movement, and scoring systems, I gained hands-on experience in handling game objects, scripting behaviors, and fine-tuning player interactions. While a small project, it was a crucial step in building my confidence with Unity and coding, setting the foundation for more complex game development.
