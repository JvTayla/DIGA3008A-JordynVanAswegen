Manga Vault Style Guide

By Jordyn Van Aswegen & Group 3

Posted: March 24, 2025

Manga Vault Style Guide Project

For our group project, we worked on creating a comprehensive style guide for Manga Vault, a Johannesburg-based specialty store dedicated to manga and African comics. Our brief was to design a brand that reflects Manga Vault's focus on rare editions, events, and Afromanga discussions. The project was a fantastic way to improve our own style guides and branding skills.

As a team, we conducted in-depth research into similar brands and their websites, identifying key trends in design, colour schemes, and overall aesthetics. We worked together to create a cohesive brand identity, from typography choices to logo designs. This experience was an excellent opportunity to collaborate and get hands-on with a live project.

s

Our team included:

  • Jordyn Van Aswegen
  • Elri Greyling
  • Ali Raffee
  • Lubabalo Dlwathi
  • Amina Moosa

The style guide we created included detailed sections on typography, colour palette, imagery, and overall brand voice. We also focused on ensuring that the design elements would work well across various digital and print media, maintaining consistency across all touchpoints.

This project was a highlight for me, as I thoroughly enjoyed collaborating with the team and refining our skills in branding and design. It was incredibly rewarding to see our collective efforts come together to create something so polished and professional. Below are a few presentation slides showcasing our work:

Overall, I found this project to be an invaluable experience. The team's collaboration was excellent, and I loved every minute of creating the Manga Vault style guide. We were able to bring our individual design ideas together to create a unified vision for the brand, which was both exciting and rewarding.
