Week 6 - Current Style Guide

By Jordyn Van Aswegen

Posted: March 24, 2025

Iteration 1

Overview:

The style guide for my portfolio website is inspired by a clean, comic book aesthetic, designed to be both visually striking and user-friendly. My goal is to create a site that reflects my artistic passion while also ensuring that the design is intuitive, easy to navigate, and professional. I want the site to have bold, dynamic colors, smooth rounded corners, and a minimalistic yet engaging layout that pulls from comic book influences without overwhelming the user.

Typography:

  • Primary Font - 'Roboto'

    I’ve chosen Roboto for most of the body text and navigation. It's a clean, versatile sans-serif font that’s easy to read on both mobile and desktop platforms. It complements the modern, minimalist style I’m aiming for and ensures that the website remains readable and functional.

    View Roboto

  • Secondary Font - 'Bangers'

    To bring in the comic book vibe, Bangers will be used for headings, titles, and impactful statements. It has a playful, bold style reminiscent of comic book lettering, which helps convey the energetic feel I'm after. It’s a great contrast to Roboto’s simplicity and adds a fun element to the design.

    View Bangers

  • Fallback Font - 'Arial'

    As a backup, I’m using Arial to ensure that the website maintains its readability and appearance across different devices and browsers. It's a safe, widely supported font that works well with both Roboto and Bangers.

    View Arial

Color Palette:

Primary Colors:

  • Deep Red (#A81000): This bold red serves as a focal point, drawing attention to key elements like headings, buttons, and calls to action. It’s vibrant and energetic, giving the site a dynamic feel without being overpowering.
  • Electric Blue (#3498DB): A bright, contrasting blue used for interactive elements such as hyperlinks, buttons, and hover effects, making the website feel more engaging.

Secondary Colors:

  • Bright Yellow (#F1C40F): A fun, eye-catching color used sparingly to highlight important details like icons, tags, or links. It adds an extra pop to the design.
  • Slate Gray (#292f34): Used for background elements and text, this neutral tone helps balance out the vibrancy of other colors and increases readability.

Neutral Colors:

White (#FFFFFF): Clean and simple, used for the main background to keep the site feeling open and airy.

Logo and Personal Branding:

For my personal logo, I’ve created a digital self-portrait that will be prominently displayed on the website. This stylized digital image represents me and serves as both a personal and professional brand mark. It helps create a unique identity and will be placed in the header for visual consistency.

Layout and Design Elements:

  • Rounded Corners:

    Used on buttons, containers, and cards to create a soft, modern aesthetic that complements the comic-inspired layout.

  • Whitespace and Hierarchy:

    Prioritizing whitespace avoids clutter and helps guide the user’s attention using visual hierarchy through font size, color, and placement.

  • Mobile Optimization:

    The mobile layout mirrors the color palette and typography but simplifies navigation using a collapsible menu and larger buttons.

  • Interactive Elements:

    Comic-inspired pop-up effects and hover transitions will make the browsing experience more dynamic and immersive.

Inspiration Website

Alex Fisher’s Portfolio Website

While designing my portfolio and style guide, I drew heavy inspiration from Alex Fisher’s website. Their minimalist layout, clean typography, and content categorization are elements I plan to adapt. I especially appreciated the strong visual organization and professional aesthetic without feeling corporate.

One feature I’m replicating is how Alex categorizes portfolio pieces into clearly defined areas. I’ll apply this to showcase my work across game design, animation, and digital art while keeping a comic-inspired visual structure.

The site’s clean layout, bold typography, and clever use of whitespace really stood out to me. My goal is to take these lessons and layer in my own dynamic, expressive twist.

Future Developments with CSS:

As I move forward with CSS development, I plan to refine spacing (padding, margins), implement responsive design rules, and experiment with comic-inspired CSS animations. These may include hover transitions and comic panel-style scroll effects.

I want to strike a balance between personality and performance—ensuring the site remains user-friendly, polished, and expressive. This style guide sets the tone for how I’ll evolve my website as both a personal and professional space.

Updates

Refinements and Updates:

In Iteration 2, I focused on translating my style guide into actual HTML and CSS to begin shaping my portfolio site. During this process, I re-evaluated layout decisions, adjusted some elements based on how they rendered in-browser, and made slight changes to typography sizing and spacing for better readability and consistency. The deep red and electric blue still form the visual identity of the site, but I experimented with hover animations and buttons to bring more interactivity to the experience.

Style Guide Application:

  • Fonts:

    I successfully implemented 'Roboto' as the body font and 'Bangers' for my headers using Google Fonts. I adjusted heading weights and sizes to enhance hierarchy and prevent them from looking too heavy on smaller screens. Arial was set as the fallback, ensuring consistency across devices.

  • Colors:

    Color was applied throughout the navigation, links, call-to-action buttons, and section dividers. Deep red (#A81000) continues to anchor key elements like headers and the navigation bar. Electric blue (#3498DB) and bright yellow (#F1C40F) are used to highlight interactive components and important tags. Slate gray is used for paragraph text for contrast and clarity.

  • Logo & Branding:

    The stylized digital self-portrait I created is now integrated in the site header. I ensured that its placement is consistent across both mobile and desktop views to reinforce my branding.

Design Decisions & Enhancements in Wireframes:

  • Header Design:

    The header now includes a logo, navigation bar, and a comic-style splash title that adjusts to screen width. I applied drop shadows to give elements a layered, comic book feel while maintaining legibility.

  • Cards & Containers:

    I created reusable content cards with rounded corners and bold headers to house project previews. These cards feature hover transitions with scale effects and a subtle comic-style border shadow.

  • Navigation:

    Navigation uses flexbox to ensure responsiveness, with a hamburger menu on smaller devices. Hover states include bold underline effects and color transitions between deep red and electric blue.

  • Buttons:

    Buttons feature comic-inspired outlines, a slight bounce animation on hover, and color transitions. Their styling is consistent across the homepage and internal pages.

Challenges:

One of the main challenges was maintaining the comic book aesthetic without overloading the user visually. I had to find the right balance between boldness and minimalism. Fine-tuning the spacing, especially on mobile, took trial and error to get right. Typography scaling across breakpoints was also tricky but now feels consistent and readable.

Final Adjustments & Decisions

While I initially planned to implement a lot of extra interactive features and animations in this version, I ended up stripping a few of them out. Some ideas—like scroll-triggered comic transitions and layered motion backgrounds—just didn’t feel right in practice. Instead, I focused on fine-tuning the feel of the site and making sure everything worked smoothly across pages.

I did, however, create custom headings for each page to keep things visually interesting and unique. They bring in comic-style energy without overwhelming the viewer. I also updated my colour palette to better reflect a comic-inspired aesthetic that’s still web-friendly. My original colours were too bright on screen and honestly kind of hurt to look at for long, so I toned them down for accessibility and overall vibe. These are my updated colours:

  • #3700ff (deep comic blue)
  • #a81000 (rich red)
  • #616161 (neutral slate)
  • #ff0000 (classic bold red)
  • #ffe600 (comic yellow)
  • Plus black, white, and filler colours where necessary

I also made sure all the page formats and sections match more closely now, which gives the site a much more cohesive, polished look. Buttons are no longer overly comic-book styled; instead, they’re simple but still tie into the theme through the colour scheme. They feel more modern but still on-brand.

One idea I had early on was creating a custom illustrated background, but after testing it, it just didn’t work. It felt cluttered and took attention away from the actual content. Surprisingly, the clean white space actually makes everything pop nicely, so I’ve left it out for now—and I might just keep it that way!

I also refined some of the site’s structural elements. I changed the navbar from a hover drop-down to a click-based drop-down. Personally, I always have issues navigating hover menus—they glitch or disappear too quickly—so I went with something more stable. That said, I’m open to feedback and can switch it back if users prefer hover interactions.

Another part I’m really happy with is the footer. It includes links to all the pages on the site and has a structure I find really clean and effective. It wraps everything up nicely and helps with easy navigation, especially on mobile.

Lastly, I ran into some styling issues when pushing to GitHub. For some reason, my style sheet wasn’t always being picked up properly, which caused a few design elements to break. It’s something I had to troubleshoot quite a bit—relinking style sheets, clearing caches, and adjusting folder structures. I'm still double-checking that everything loads consistently across different devices and browsers.

Overall, this version feels way more refined, intentional, and functional compared to Iteration 1. It’s a balance between personality and usability, which is what I was aiming for.
